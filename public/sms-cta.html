SMS Consent CTA Guidance

This page provides recommended SMS consent disclosure language for businesses using Tradesman Systems. It is not a consumer opt-in form and does not collect customer information.

For reviewers and business owners: Use the sample wording below on your own website, forms, and listings. Customers opt in on your properties or by contacting your business directly—not on this guidance page.

Recommended SMS Consent Disclosure

Businesses using Tradesman Systems may place the following disclosure near website contact forms, estimate request forms, service request forms, online listings, advertisements, and other customer intake points:

“By submitting a service request or contacting [Business Name], you agree to receive SMS messages related to your inquiry, scheduling, estimates, job updates, and customer support from [Business Name]. Message frequency varies. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out. Reply HELP for help.”

Customers may also opt in by directly calling, texting, or otherwise contacting a business regarding a service request. SMS messages must be one-to-one, service-related, and connected to the customer’s inquiry.

Tradesman Systems does not support bulk messaging or unsolicited outreach. Businesses are responsible for obtaining and maintaining customer consent before sending messages.
